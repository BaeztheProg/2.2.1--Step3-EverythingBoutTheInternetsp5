How the Internet Changed the World—and How It Will Keep Changing It

Rhea Raj, Yukthika Palavelli, Aastha Nanda, Amulya Medarametla Febuarary 13, 2025 Technology

The internet is a global network of computers and devices that allows people to access information and communicate with others worldwide. It connects millions of systems, enabling everything from browsing and socializing to online shopping and cloud computing. This interconnected infrastructure continues to evolve, shaping how we live, work, and interact with technology. Reading this article to learn more about the internet.

The internet is home to 5.18 billion users worldwide.

The History

The internet was created in the late 1960s by the U.S. Department of Defense’s ARPA through the development of ARPANET. The project was led by Bob Taylor, with contributions from researchers like Vinton Cerf and Robert Kahn, who later developed key protocols. The first successful connection was made in 1969 between four universities.

It was created primarily for military purposes, including the establishment of a secure communication network during the Cold War. The goal was to develop a decentralized system that could withstand attacks and maintain communication across military and government channels. Additionally, it was designed to allow researchers at universities and government institutions to easily share information and collaborate on projects, streamlining communication in the academic and scientific communities..

Impact and Use

The internet is used for global communication and collaboration, connecting people across the world. It serves as a vast resource for learning and education, offering access to information on countless topics. It is also a platform for sharing news, staying informed, and transferring money internationally, making it essential for both personal and business use. The internet has revolutionized communication, learning, and business, making information and global connections more accessible than ever. It has created new opportunities for innovation but also raised concerns about privacy, security, and inequality, highlighting the need for responsible use.

Numerous websites and apps have been introduced over the years though the internet and its webpages.

What's Next

In the future, the internet will become more immersive and interactive, powered by technologies like AR, VR, and blockchain. Users will experience a seamless blend of augmented, virtual, and physical realities, allowing digital environments to interact with the real world in new and exciting ways.
